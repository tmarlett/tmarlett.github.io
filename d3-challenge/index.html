Income vs Smokers in the United States

Correlation Discovered Between Smoking and Income

Using data containing the amount of smokers in each state by percent of its population, as well as the average income of each state, we have created a chart to display the correlation between income and people's tendency to smoke.

We can observe a clear trend in lower-income states having the most smokers per capita, with higher-income states sitting comfortably at under 20% of its population being smokers. A single outlier (Utah) exists and is sitting comfortably at around $60,000 average income and just under 10% of its population being smokers.
